The Influence of Chinese Culture


Recent years, China offers the greatest growth potential for international culture. Different cultural expressions such as Chinese opera, kung fu, calligraphy, Confucius Institute, paintings have become more and more popular to people all around the world. The trend of internationalization of the Chinese culture shows a great vitality of chinese culture. This workshop based on some great previous work, however mainly focus on the visualization, in order to exhibit a more popular, more intuitive and clear result. Basiclly this people-friendly web site mean to quantitively show you differnet aspects of chinese culture, let people know the development of chinese culture better and easier and attract more people to engaged in this field. For your convenience, all the visualized data or relevant data source and the link of relevant previous work can be got directly. You may use these for future analysis. What's more, the core culture values reflected from different culture embodiments should be what we cherish most.Although there exist some differences between the Mainland China, Hong Kong, and Taiwan, the core culture values are shared by Chinese People no matter where they are.



Abstract

The purpose of our work is to exhibit the influence of chinese culture from different aspects in different levels. Previous work like (Michel et al. science(2011)) investigated the culture trend quantitively based on digitized books and (Schich Maximilian, et al. science(2014)) focused on the emergent processes driving cultural history. Basiclly, most of these works are famous and successful, but wide. Our work based on these previous work but more focus on chinese culture development and influence. What's more, our target audience are normal people they may not have enough specialized knowledge to understand what the great previous work do and what the results mean. Thus, there are many plots included in this website to intuitively show the power of chinese culture in different aspects and in order to make visitors more clear about the plots, there are some brief analysis followed each graph.

World Heritage

Heritage is the divine creation of predecessors, what we live with today and what we pass on to future generations. As crystallizaiton of wisdom, heritage not only shows cultural accumulation, but also importantly it reflects the influence of different cultures. In this part, we will explore the dataset contains information of world heritage from year 1978 to year 2015. The dataset has been collected from the site maintained by the international World Heritage Programme administered by the UNESCO(United Nations Education, Scientific and Cultural Organization) World Heritage Committee and it contains 1031 sites: 802 cultural, 197 natural, and 32 mixed properties in 163 states parties. The top countries to the greatest number of World Heritage according to the sites is Italy(51 sites), China(48 sites), Spain(44 sites), France(41 sites), Germany(40 sites), Mexico(33 sites), and India(32 sites). The following of this part, we provide you a symbol map showing all the sites distributed all around the world, so you can clearly see that Europe contributes most of the heritage sites, followed by Asia, America, Africa etc. Colors represent different years the heritages have been inscribed, that is the color of site more close to red, the inscribed date is more recent. Crossfilter technique has been used in this symbol map to provide you some ''freedom''. If you want to see the result of a specific range of year, just choose the corresponding years and the symbol map will show you the result simultaneously.

Book-Crossing

BookCrossing(also know as BC, BCing or BXing) refer to the practice of leaving a book in a public place to be picked up and read by others. With the help of Internet(Book-Crossing website), different kinds of books can be transfer to other countries. Those transferred books show a message of friendship and love on one hand, and on the other hand they also promote the dissemination of culture. In this part, we focus on the dataset collected by Cai-Nicolas Ziegler using a crawler to crawl 4-week data from the Book-Crossing website. The dataset contains 278,858 users who provides 1,149,780 ratings about 271,379 books.(more detailed information please refer to Book-Crossing Dataset)

Data Information

The Book-Crossing dataset contains three tables, i.e. BX-Users, BX-Books, BX-Book-Ratings

  • BX-Users:

    Contains the users. Note that user IDs('User-ID') have been map to some integers for user privacy. Demographic data is offered('Location', 'Age') if available. Otherwise, these fields contain NULL-values.

  • BX-Books:

    Contains information of books. Books are identified by their respective ISBN, and also some content-based information is given('Book-Title', 'Book-Author', 'Year-Of-Publication', 'Publisher'), obtained from Amazon Web Services. URLs linking to cover images are also given, appearing in three different flavours('Image-URL-S', 'Image-URL-M', 'Image-URL-L'), i.e., small, medium, and large.

  • BX-Books-Ratings:

    Contains the book rating information. Ratings('Book-Rating') are either, expressed on a scale from 1-10(higher values denoting higher apperciation), or implicit, expressed by 0.

The following 3D graph visualizes the statistic number and distribution of Book-Crossing website users. The range of original values is too large, so we map the original values to new values with a small range. The mapping function can be seen as a weighted piecewise linear function. Let's say the original value is , then the mapping function can be expressed as:

f(x) = \frac{1}{5} \times \begin{cases} \frac{x}{10} & x < 10 \\ \frac{1}{90} \cdot x + \frac{8}{9} & 10 \leq x < 100 \\ \frac{1}{1000} \cdot x + 1.9 & x \geq 100 \\ \end{cases}.
